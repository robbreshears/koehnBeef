Frequently Asked Questions


How much meat will I get in a quarter of beef?


Each animal is different, but the average amount of meat in a quarter is around 120-130 pounds.

How much of each cut will I get in a quarter or an eighth?


A quarter or an eighth both have approximately:

  • 40% ground beef (1 lb/packages)
  • 25% steaks (New York, Rib, Sirloin, Tenderloins)
  • 35% roasts (arm, chuck, tenderized round) and miscellaneous cuts (stew meat, short ribs)

How much freezer space do I need for my beef?


An eighth of a beef will fit in 75-80% of the freezer part of a refrigerator with a top, bottom, or side freezer section. A quarter of beef will fit in 50-75% of a small chest freezer.

How long will a quarter of beef last?


The beef will be fine for a year if it stays frozen. How long it will last your family depends on the size of your family and how often you eat beef. You might find that you eat it more often when you can just walk to your freezer instead of going to the grocery store!

What percent lean is the hamburger I will get from my beef?


Our beef is approximately 90% lean.

How does the price for 1/8 or 1/4 of a beef compare with the price in the grocery store?


The average cost for grass-fed beef in the grocery store is $5.99/lb for ground beef. Other cuts range from $9.99 to $19.99 per pound. Our beef is approximately $7.25/lb and includes steaks, roasts, and ground beef.
